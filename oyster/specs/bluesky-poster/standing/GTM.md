# GTM Report: bluesky-poster

## Target Audience

The initial target audience for **bluesky-poster** will be tech-savvy individuals and early adopters who are active on decentralized social media platforms. Specifically, the first 100 users will likely be:

- **Developers and Tech Enthusiasts**: Individuals interested in the AT Protocol and Python, looking to experiment with new technologies.
- **Content Creators**: Users who frequently post on social media and are looking for innovative ways to manage and distribute their content.
- **Decentralization Advocates**: People passionate about decentralized systems and blockchain technology, eager to try out new decentralized applications.
- **Early Adopters of Bluesky**: Users who are already part of the Bluesky community and are interested in integrating new tools with their existing workflows.

## Distribution Channel

Users will primarily discover **bluesky-poster** through the following channels:

- **Social Media Platforms**: Leveraging existing decentralized social media platforms like Bluesky to create buzz and share updates.
- **Tech Forums and Communities**: Engaging with communities on Reddit, Hacker News, and specialized forums like Stack Overflow and GitHub.
- **Developer Conferences and Meetups**: Participating in events focused on Python, AT Protocol, and decentralized technologies.
- **Word of Mouth**: Encouraging early adopters to share their experiences with peers and on their social networks.
- **Newsletter and Blogs**: Publishing regular updates and tutorials on a dedicated blog and through a newsletter to keep the community informed.

## Success Metrics

The following KPIs will be used to measure product-market fit:

1. **User Growth Rate**: Achieving a steady increase in the number of active users, aiming for a 20% week-over-week growth in the first month.
2. **Engagement Metrics**: Tracking the number of posts created, interactions (likes, shares, comments), and session duration to ensure users are actively using the platform.
3. **Net Promoter Score (NPS)**: Measuring user satisfaction and likelihood to recommend the product to others, with a target NPS of 7 or higher.

## Launch Strategy

For a useful v0.1, the minimum viable product (MVP) will include:

- **Core Functionality**: Ability to create, edit, and delete posts using the AT Protocol.
- **Basic UI**: A simple, intuitive interface for users to interact with the application.
- **Integration with Bluesky**: Seamless connection to Bluesky for posting and retrieving content.
- **User Authentication**: Secure login and signup process using decentralized identity solutions.
- **Feedback Mechanism**: In-app feedback form to gather user insights and bug reports.

## Timeline

### Week 1:
- **Objective**: Finalize product design and development of MVP features.
- **Activities**: Complete UI/UX design, implement core functionalities, and set up basic infrastructure.

### Week 2:
- **Objective**: Conduct internal testing and bug fixing.
- **Activities**: Perform thorough testing, gather feedback from a small group of testers, and make necessary adjustments.

### Week 4:
- **Objective**: Launch the MVP and begin user acquisition.
- **Activities**: Deploy the application, initiate marketing campaigns, and start onboarding the first 100 users. Gather initial feedback and monitor KPIs to assess performance and make improvements.
