# GTM Report: agentforge

## Go-To-Market Report for AgentForge

### 1. Target Audience
The first 100 users for **AgentForge** will be tech-savvy developers and small-to-medium-sized startups focused on AI and automation. These early adopters are likely to be active on developer communities like GitHub, Stack Overflow, and Reddit's r/MachineLearning and r/Python. They are interested in tools that simplify the development of intelligent agents and are keen on experimenting with new technologies to enhance their projects or streamline workflows.

### 2. Distribution Channel
Users will primarily discover **AgentForge** through the following channels:
- **Developer Forums and Communities**: Engaging in discussions on platforms like GitHub, Stack Overflow, and Reddit.
- **Social Media**: Leveraging LinkedIn, Twitter, and Facebook groups focused on AI, Python, and React.
- **Content Marketing**: Publishing technical blogs, tutorials, and case studies on platforms like Medium and Dev.to.
- **Partnerships**: Collaborating with AI and developer tool companies for co-promotions.
- **Webinars and Meetups**: Hosting online events to showcase the capabilities and use-cases of AgentForge.

### 3. Success Metrics
The following KPIs will define the product-market fit for **AgentForge**:
1. **User Acquisition Rate**: Achieving a steady growth rate of 20% week-over-week in the number of sign-ups and active users.
2. **Engagement Metrics**: Ensuring that 50% of users return to the platform at least once a week and spend an average of 30 minutes on the platform.
3. **Customer Feedback**: Gathering qualitative feedback through surveys and interviews, aiming for a Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 8 or higher.

### 4. Launch Strategy
For a minimum viable v0.1 launch, the focus will be on the core functionalities that allow users to:
- Create, manage, and deploy intelligent agents using Python FastAPI.
- Utilize pre-built templates and modules for common AI tasks.
- Monitor and analyze agent performance through a React-based dashboard.
- Access basic documentation and tutorials to guide them through the setup and usage.

### 5. Timeline
- **Week 1**: 
  - Finalize the core features and conduct internal testing.
  - Set up the landing page and start building an email list.
  - Begin outreach to initial communities and forums.

- **Week 2**: 
  - Launch a private beta with a select group of 20 users for feedback.
  - Refine the user interface and fix any critical bugs.
  - Start content marketing with blog posts and social media teasers.

- **Week 4**: 
  - Officially launch v0.1 to the public.
  - Host a webinar to demonstrate the platform and answer questions.
  - Gather initial user feedback and make necessary adjustments.
  - Begin tracking KPIs to assess initial market reception and plan for future iterations.

By following this plan, **AgentForge** aims to establish a strong foundation and gain traction among its target audience.
